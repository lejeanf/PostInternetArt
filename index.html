A constant evolution/
The first version of internet was developped by scientist Tim Berners-Lee in 1989. This technology was used by the army and quickly got viral in the accademic world. Web Art:

Art that require coding skills. An art that museums failed to integrate in gallery spaces in the first place. Most of the net.art represented in real life involved uncomfortable interactive artworks with computers, which ended up being used only to check emails instead of experiencing the work itself. As Olia lialina said in her essay All you need is Link: "Net art exhibitions are the ugliest phenomenon of the modern art scene."

Weird in-between period:

During the early 2000s, an important variety of works laid the basis of what came to be a post-internet boom. Two of the most notable examples were the work of Cory Arcangel and Seth Price. While Ancangel was paving the way of a new conceptual way of dealing with historicity and specificity of internet as a medium inside and outsite the computer, Price writing would be proven a crucial theorical anchor for a new generation of artists. In his famous text Dispersion, he underline how artists might exist outside the limits of the art world. He then quote the philosopher Sarat Marajah, who sees the question as "a marker for ways we might be able to engage with works, events, spasms, ructions that don't look like art and don't count as art but are somehow electric, energy nodes, attractors, transmitters, conductors of new thinking, new subjectivity and action that visual artwork in the traditional sense is not able to articulate."

The New Aesthetic:

The New Aesthetic is a term, coined by James Bridle, used to refer to the increasing appearance of the visual language of digital technology and the Internet in the physical world, and the blending of virtual and physical. Bridle, James. ‚ÄúThe New Aesthetic and Its Politics.‚Äù Booktwo.org.com (blog), June 12, 2013. available here.

The New Aesthetic is a native product of modern network culture. An open-source, shareable concept, crowdsource, is made by many small pieces loosely joined. It can be work, a conversation, a performance, an experiment and more. It comments issues around computation, as we live in a society define and shaped by technology. Technology is political like everything else and we should be concerned of these, better controlling them before being controlled by them. The notion of identity

step-back-time-history-evolution-digital-identity

As the accessibiliy of internet and the increasing easyness of uploading to online, the notion of Identity changed. At first websters were refered as users, it was common pseudos for usernames making the identity behind the user less certain. Now, with social networks constantly trying to identify the actual person behind the IP, the idea of online anonymity fades away although initiatives coming from the hacker culture can be found to counter-act this trend (e.g. the use of VPN's, or the tor network). Now google even refer to this online identity as a Ledger, a knowledged passed through generations that might help understanding human behaviours on a larger scale (see this video for more information).

But how these trends affect the views and practices of today? Michael Wang argues, in a article on the Work of Ryan Trecartin, that "a representational order in which words, images and objects no longer inhabit separate strata of experience, but instead intermingle, hybridize and transform into one another". From this intensely recorded, recklessly data-ified and capitalist-post-MTV fueled milieu comes what Douglas Coupland have called the extreme present. In it's most recent incarnation, it encompass a rapid endeavor of massively networked identity politics. On the internet and beyond, many practices like the one of Ann Hirsch, Martine Syms, Keith Obadike, Bunny Rogers and Amalia Ulman have complicated the problem of the gaze, while setting up narratives and manifestos through the lens of gender, race, nation and other sociological categories.

Critic analysis and definition of criterias

Criterias helping to know what is Post-Internet-Art.

1 - Art that either in the process or in the making uses internet content or medium.
2 - Was made after 2004 (since internet became a mass information support)
3 - Internet based practice that was exhibited in the real world.
